## Professional Overview

Dionne Bunsha is an award-winning author and journalist known for her work covering human rights, social justice, and environmental issues. She served as Senior Assistant Editor for Frontline magazine in Mumbai, India, and has contributed to publications including The Guardian, The Hindu, and New Internationalist.

## Published Work

Her acclaimed non-fiction book, "Scarred: Experiments with Violence in Gujarat" (Penguin India, 2006), examines the aftermath of communal violence in Gujarat. Arundhati Roy praised it, stating "everyone ought to read it."

## Academic Background

Bunsha holds a Master's degree in Resource and Environmental Management from Simon Fraser University in Vancouver, focusing on coastal ecosystem management, and an M.Sc. in Development Studies from the London School of Economics, where she researched farmer suicides in Maharashtra.

## Notable Achievements

- Knight International Journalism Fellow at Stanford University (2008-2009)
- Ramnath Goenka Excellence in Journalism Awards for Books and Environmental Reporting (2006-2007)
- International Federation of Journalists Journalism for Tolerance Prize (2005)
- PUCL Journalism for Human Rights Award (2003)
- Pacific Institute for Climate Solutions Graduate Fellowship (2011-2012)

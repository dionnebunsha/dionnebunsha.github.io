## Book Overview

**Title:** Scarred: Experiments with Violence in Gujarat

**Author:** Dionne Bunsha

**Publisher:** Penguin India (2006)

## Description

This work examines the extensive political violence in Gujarat, particularly following the 2002 Sabarmati Express incident. The author documents the experiences of survivors and refugees whose lives were permanently altered by subsequent violence. The book also explores the perpetrators' ideologies and their vision for Gujarat's social structure, including the marginalization of Muslim communities.

The narrative addresses critical questions about the February 2002 events and the political response, offering investigative journalism combined with personal accounts of those seeking justice amid ongoing fear.

## Critical Reception

Notable endorsements include:
- Arundhati Roy: "In Scarred, Dionne Bunsha reveals the everyday fascism. This is excellent work."
- The Hindu's C.T. Kurien praised it as a meticulous, sensitive account
- India Today Book Club selected it as "Pick of the Season"
- Tehelka commended its honest, straightforward approach to documenting unhealed wounds

## Featured Articles

### "Contested Territory" (June 2015)

Published in New Internationalist, this piece examines India's rightwing government's targeting of NGOs. Bunsha explores how the state labels grassroots movements opposing corporate development projects as "anti-national," while questioning whether environmental organizations genuinely impede economic progress.

### "Author's Note for Scarred: Experiments with Violence in Gujarat" (May 2014)

An extended author's reflection on documenting the 2002 Gujarat communal violence. The note describes her investigation into both victims in relief camps and perpetrators, revealing how ordinary people became conditioned for violence through ideological training while leaders made cynical calculations.

### "Teesta's Tears" (May 2014)

Reporting from Sikkim on indigenous Lepcha communities' resistance to hydroelectric dam projects. The article chronicles young activists on hunger strikes to protect sacred lands and biodiversity, revealing tensions between development promises and environmental/cultural destruction.

### "What Clayoquot Sound Faces Now" (October 2013)

An analysis of conservation efforts protecting old-growth temperate rainforests in British Columbia, examining innovative partnerships between First Nations and environmental organizations to prevent logging of intact watersheds.

### "Gone with the Waves" (October 2013)

Photojournalism documenting coastal erosion in Gujarat villages where fishing families face displacement due to rising sea levels linked to climate change, exploring both global warming impacts and local environmental degradation.

### "The Holes in Our Chappals" (June 2007)

Investigation into farmer suicides in Gujarat, revealing how the state suppresses statistics to maintain the chief minister's narrative of agricultural prosperity while rural communities face debt-driven desperation.

## Thematic Focus Areas

Bunsha's journalism concentrates on:
- Environmental degradation and climate impacts
- Indigenous rights and land dispossession
- State violence and communal conflicts
- Agricultural crises and farmer welfare
- NGO activism and civil society
- Human rights documentation

## Professional Context

Bunsha is an award-winning journalist dividing her time between Mumbai and Vancouver, former senior editor at Frontline magazine, and author of the book "Scarred: Experiments with Violence in Gujarat" (Penguin India).
